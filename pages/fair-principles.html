FAIR Principles

''Transparency, collaboration, accelerated research, and better decision-making are all facilitated by data sharing, which advances science.''

FAIR stands for "Findable, Accessible, Interoperable and Re-usable". The main goal of the FAIR principles is to enable the 'long term care' of valuable digital assets. The scope of these principles goes beyond the data in the conventional sense. It includes all the components of the research process, including the algorithms and workflows that lead to the resulting data.

Why is this important?

Through the use of unique identifiers and the formal shared knowledge representation, the FAIR Data Principles make data more valuable by making it simpler to mix and integrate. As a result of machines' ability to comprehend the origin and subject matter of data, such data is simpler to reuse, repurpose, and share. Additionally, it fosters collaboration, quickens research, and makes reuse in science easier.

How to make data FAIR?

Image Source

Findable:

The first step in (re)using data is to find them. Metadata and data should be easy to find for both humans and computers.

  • data are assigned a globally unique and eternally persistent identifier.
  • data are registered or indexed in a searchable resource.
Accessible:

Once the user finds the required data, they need to know how they can be accessed, possibly including authentication and authorisation.

  • data are retrievable by their identifier using a standardised communications protocol.
  • data are accessible, even when the data are no longer available.
Interoperable:

The data usually need to be integrated with other data. The data need to interoperate with applications or workflows for analysis, storage, and processing.

  • data use a formal, accessible, shared, and broadly applicable language for knowledge representation.
  • data use vocabularies that follow FAIR principles.
Reusable:

Data reuse is FAIR's ultimate objective. This can be accomplished by having well-described metadata and data, which can then be integrated in various contexts.

  • data are richly described with a plurality of accurate and relevant attributes
  • data use vocabularies that follow FAIR principles.
