Open Hardware

"Open hardware or open source hardware, refers to the design specifications of a physical object which are licensed in such a way that said object can be studied, modified, created, and distributed by anyone. Open hardware is a set of design principles and legal practices, not a specific type of object. The term can therefore refer to any number of objects—like automobiles, chairs, computers, robots, or even houses." [OpenSource]↩

Image by: Mateo Zlatar from Open Source Hardware Association.

Open Hardware License

Open source hardware licenses allows recipients of designs and documentations to study them, modify them and distribute them or any modifications. [OpenSource]↩

It generally does not prevent someone from selling or giving out the project's documentation. [OpenSource]↩

How Different is Open Hardware from other Hardware?

Every open hardware must have a documentation with design files and its source code. [OpenSource]↩

It is very easy to reproduce open hardware unlike closed hardware where reproduction is very difficult because of patent laws. [OpenSource]↩

How Different is Open Source Hardware from Open Source Software?

Open hardware projects are focused on production of physical objects which involves financial investment for physical materials and labour. [OpenSource]↩

Open software projects can distribute its software as free but due to financial involvement in open hardware projects, it is not feasible to distribute physical components. [OpenSource]↩
