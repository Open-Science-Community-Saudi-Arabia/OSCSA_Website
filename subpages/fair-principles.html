What is FAIR Principle?

Scientific Data issued the FAIR Guiding Principles for scientific data management and stewardship in 2016. The authors wanted to offer recommendations for enhancing digital assets' Findability, Accessibility, Interoperability, and Reuse. Accordind to Wikipedia, Data that adhere to the findability, accessibility, interoperability, and reusability criteria are known as FAIR data (FAIR). Because humans increasingly rely on computational support to deal with data as a result of the increase in volume, complexity, and creation speed of data, the principles emphasize machine-actionability (i.e., the capacity of computational systems to find, access, interoperate, and reuse data with neither full nor minimal human intervention).

Findable

Finding the data is the initial stage in its (re)use. Data and metadata ought to be simple to find for both computers and people. The automatic discovery of datasets and services depends on machine-readable metadata, hence this is a crucial step in the FAIRification process.

Accessible

After locating the necessary data, the user must be aware of how to access it, which may involve authentication and authorization.

Interoperable

Usually, the data must be combined with additional data. Additionally, the data must work with workflows or apps for analysis, storing, and processing.

Reusable

FAIR's main objective is to maximize data reuse. To accomplish this, metadata and data must be adequately specified in order to be duplicated and/or integrated in many contexts.
